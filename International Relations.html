Inter-Governmental Coordination and International Relations

Inter-Governmental Coordination and International Relations in the Digital Age

The internet facilitates collaboration between local, national, and international governmental bodies.

Inter-Governmental Coordination and International Relations

The internet supports coordinated responses to global challenges like pandemics and climate change.

Examples

  • Online summits (e.g., UN, G7 meetings)
  • Data sharing during crises (e.g., COVID-19)
  • EU-wide systems for immigration, justice, and security

The Use of the Internet in Government

The internet enables faster inter-agency communication, reduces travel and administrative costs, and facilitates real-time coordination in emergencies.

Benefits

  • Faster inter-agency communication
  • Reduced travel and administrative costs
  • Real-time coordination in emergencies

Challenges

  • Data interoperability across nations
  • Cybersecurity risks in cross-border data exchange
  • Diplomatic and political tensions
